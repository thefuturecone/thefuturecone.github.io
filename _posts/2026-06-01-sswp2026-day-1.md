The first day of [ICTS-SSWP 2026](https://www.icts.res.in/program/SWWP2026)[^1] began with registrations, as all of us arrived with enthusiasm and anticipation for the program. Nearly 50 undergraduate women students from different institutions took part in this 10-day residential program, and the excitement was evident from the very beginning as everyone looked forward to learning, interacting, and exploring new ideas.

A big thanks to the organizers and student volunteers, who made sure everything was in place. Their efforts helped the program run smoothly and created a welcoming atmosphere for everyone.

The formal program commenced with an inspiring talk by [**Dr. Rajesh Gopakumar**](https://www.icts.res.in/people/rajesh-gopakumar), Director of [ICTS](https://www.icts.res.in). He spoke about the various initiatives and programs at [ICTS](https://www.icts.res.in) and highlighted the broader educational and research initiatives undertaken by [TIFR](https://www.tifr.res.in). His talk provided us with valuable insights into opportunities available in science and research, especially for women.

This was followed by welcome addresses from [Prof. Shirish Pathare](https://www.hbcse.tifr.res.in/people/scientific/shirish-r-pathare) from [HBCSE](https://www.hbcse.tifr.res.in/) and [Prof. Kripa Gowrishankar](https://azimpremjiuniversity.edu.in/people/kripa-gowrishankar) from [Azim Premji University](https://azimpremjiuniversity.edu.in), Bengaluru. They gave us an overview of their respective institutions; shared the vision behind the Summer School for Women in Physics (SSWP); and spoke about the goals and expectations of the program.

The talks and the ice-breaker sessions throughout the day were informative, inspirational, and highly engaging. They set a good start for the days ahead and encouraged us to make the most of the opportunities offered by the program.

With a welcoming start and a room full of eager learners, the first day marked the beginning of what promised to be an exciting journey for all us.
